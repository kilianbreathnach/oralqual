Image Modelling with Astrometry.net

with David Hogg and Dustin Lang (CMU)

Astrometry.net

  • Compares sources found in image to a catalogue with "geometric hashing"
    • Super fast and ZERO false positives
  • Can be used to astrometrically calibrate any astronomical image (optical)
  • Crowdsourcing: currently ~50k pictures tagged #astrometry on Flickr

But...

  • Calibration only achieves accurate positions of astronomical sources
  • We would also like to know about source brightnesses - enables more science
  • Calibration done using an imperfect catalogue (USNO-B)

Idea

  • Construct Image model with an image catalogue of stars, galaxies, background... Prior knowledge about model from the output of Astrometry.net code
  • Use model to generate a fake data image and compare with real data
  • Improve the comparison by including missing stars, motion of stars, supernovae... Science!
  • Do this for many images to build up a probabilistic catalogue for the sky

Goals of the Project

  • Cosmology
    • Determine useful/interesting things about cosmological models from redshift survey data
    • Improve constraints on cosmological parameters with new data and techniques
  • Dark Sector
    • Model dark matter and galaxy bias better
    • Study accelerated expansion and its nature

Why?

  • Who doesn't want to know the processes regarding our fundamental origin??
  • We're in an era where the data just keep getting better and someone has to find out what they're telling us!
  • Better data requires the development of better and cleverer techniques and perspectives to deal with it

Large Scale Structure

  • Assuming homogeneity, isotropy, and GR hold, we are given theoretical predictions for the structure of matter in the universe
  • Dark Matter simulations assume collisionless cold dark matter to give halo distributions after time evolution
  • Use this to predict a galaxy distribution and compare with the data

The HOD: What is it and why bother?

  • Used to populate N-body dark matter simulations with galaxies
  • Takes a halo catalogue and populates each halo with a number of galaxies dependent only on the mass of the halo
  • Succesfully reproduces observable clustering statistics as seen in galaxy surveys! A robust method to account for non-linear galaxy bias, cool!



Image source: Zehavi et al. (2011)

What HOD tells us about Cosmology

  • Using a Halo Model from a given Cosmology, models nonlinear bias of galaxy clustering
  • Marginalises over and is thus insensitive to models of galaxy evolution
  • By studying growth of structure and comparing with expansion history results, can break degeneracy between different Cosmological models [Linder 2005]

Void Probability Function

  • Probability that a randomly placed sphere in space, of a given radius, contains no galaxies
  • e.g. for a Poisson distribution of points, \( P_{0} (r) = P(k = 0, r) = \frac{\lambda^{k}}{k!} e^{-\lambda r} = e^{-\lambda r} \)
  • Contains information about higher order correlators in the galaxy clustering. Importantly, contains complementary info to 2-point function: breaks bias-cosmology degeneracy.
